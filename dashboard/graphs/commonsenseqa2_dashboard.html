CommonsenseQA 2.0 is an enhanced benchmark designed to rigorously evaluate machine commonsense reasoning capabilities through carefully crafted multiple-choice questions. The dataset contains 14,000 questions spanning 1,417 ConceptNet topics, ensuring comprehensive coverage of commonsense domains while minimizing answer overlap to prevent memorization.

Questions are crowdsourced with sophisticated distractors derived from semantically related concepts in ConceptNet's knowledge graph. This design rigorously tests models' ability to perform implicit reasoning and demonstrate genuine world understanding beyond simple pattern matching or surface-level associations.

The Score metric (0-100%) measures accuracy on these commonsense inference tasks. As a key benchmark in natural language understanding, CommonsenseQA 2.0 continues to reveal persistent challenges in large language models' grasp of everyday knowledge, causal reasoning, temporal understanding, and contextual inference.
